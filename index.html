Cognivise is a real-time AI tutor that runs alongside any learning content — YouTube videos, uploaded files, or screen share. It monitors the learner through webcam and microphone, classifies cognitive state every 15 seconds across 7 learning states, and dispatches one of 8 intervention types with precision. Agent Algsoch (powered by Gemini 2.5 Realtime) knows your name, tracks mastery per topic, schedules spaced repetition, and never gets tired.

Why This Session is Critical for Cognivise

01
Frame-by-frame vision is Cognivise's unsolved frontier

My long-term goal is monitoring everything — environment, movement, micro-expression, frame by frame — not just aggregate scores. This session shows how Nvidia builds production visual AI pipelines at scale. I need batching, scheduling, and latency patterns no paper covers.

02
Closing the observe → reason → adapt loop in real time

Cognivise classifies 7 learning states every 15 seconds. Bringing that to sub-second visual feedback — where the agent reacts to a single confused frame — is the next milestone. Visual AI Agents architecture is the missing blueprint.

03
Jetson AGX Thor = offline AI tutoring for Indian classrooms

Most learners I want to reach have no reliable cloud. A Jetson AGX Thor lets me run FaceMesh + YOLO11 + Gemini Flash entirely on-device — transforming Cognivise from cloud-dependent into an offline edge AI tutor for low-connectivity schools.

04
Brev credits unlock the next Cognivise milestone

Fine-tuning the question generation model on real interaction data requires GPU hours I can't currently afford as an IIT Madras student. $100 Brev credits directly unblocks mastery-calibrated adaptive difficulty — the last 40% of the adaptation layer.
